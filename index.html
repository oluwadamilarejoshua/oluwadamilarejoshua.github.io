Hi, welcome to my page!

I am a Research and Teaching Assistant in the Department of Statistics at the Federal University of Technology, Akure (FUTA), primarily mentored by Professor O. A. Adeoti and Dr. O. O. Ojo. My research interests include applied time-series analysis, Bayesian inference, and machine learning, with relevant applications in finance and economics. I am also interested in exploring topics related to mental health epidemiology.

Macroeconomic stability, financial decision-making, and mental health outcomes are some of the topics that pique my interest in studying the potential of advanced statistical methods to reveal trends and generate effective solutions. My work aims to connect rigorous statistics with problems that happen in the real world by adopting methods that make systems easier to understand when they are complicated and changing, models more stable, and predictions more accurate. My journey shows how much I want to use data-driven insights to solve problems across disciplines, which will lead to new ways of dealing with social and economic issues.
